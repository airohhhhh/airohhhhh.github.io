Redran

A powerful pet that's held back by an extremely conditional personality.

Redran’s preferred personality, Rowdy, boosts its damage by up to 50%. Redran lives and dies by this Personality - if you can score lots of last hits, Redran will be immensly powerful, but without it, Redran does extremely mediocore damage. Wouldn't recommend trying to use this in most multiplayer content - without its personality up and running, Redran can be slow to snag the initial kills.

Redran is best used in long quests with plenty of mobs for Redran to feed off of, preferably solo quests so you needn't fight other players for kills - so it's no surprise that Redran's domain is the two Endless Quests - Memories Beyond, and Eternal Rondo.

Rowdy / Rambunctious

Increases damage dealt by 2% every time Redran kills an enemy, up to a max of 50% at 25 kills.

Redran's preferred base personality.

Hawkish / Belligerent

Increase damage based on the number of nearby enemies. (6% for 1-4 enemies, 12% for 5+ enemies).

Rock (14★)

Rowdy + Hawkish all rolled into one. Also, 5% RDR.

Redran's finalized personality.

The box for Rondo is a bit weird. You might look at this box and think "Hey, wouldn't Redran be quite squishy if Invincibility Roll isn't active?" and you'd be correct.

It is a roll we're forced to run, though. Rondo frequently features the mission order "Don't Take Damage", and that's a problem since we take damage when our pets get hit during Alter Ego.

You'll need to activate Julius Nykta at least every couple minutes to insure that Redran is invincible as much as possible - to deal with the aforementioned stage orders, but also because Redran has no other defensive rolls to speak of. Redran's made of paper without Invicibility Roll procs.

Redran Laser (25 PP)
Press and hold to lock on to up to 8 enemies or enemy parts, and let go to launch projectiles.

This is by far Redran's most useful and versatile PA.

You can use it to hit a boss with many parts, or a spread out crowd of mobs. You can also quickly collect lock ons from a crowd of enemies, and then release them while Redran is facing a boss or mini boss without many parts, to cheat out damage with this PA.

This PA is really strong, so in any situation where you can use it effectively it's generally worth it to do so.

This PA has one big caveat, though - if you cast Laser while you lose target of an enemy, Redran will become stuck; you'll be charged the PP for the PA, but it'll deal zero damage. Keep an eye out for that!



Redran Slash (5 PP)
Redran dashes in a circle dealing damage and knocking enemies down. Redran is also invincible during this PA.

This is a good mobbing tool for hitting grouped crowds of enemies, or in any situation where you need to get out damage for a low PP cost.



Redran Mine (10 PP)
Places a marker on an enemy that can later be detonated by Redran Laser, Redran Gravity, or Redran Burst. Upon detonation, Redran Mine will create a sphere that deals damage over time for a few seconds.

This PA has a few uses! Firstly, Mine > Gravity can be used to group mobs together and deal damage to them.

It also serves as a good way to make use of the downtime that exists between repeated casts of Laser or Burst - provided you're not too close to your target(s), Laser > Mine will detonate despite Mine being casted after Laser, resulting in a super effective general damage rotation.



Redran Gravity (15 PP)
Create a gravitational field drawing in enemies in a small area.

Given that this PA does zero damage, you'll usually want to do the Mine > Gravity combo mentioned above unless you really need the suction and don't have time to fit in a Mine.



Redran Burst (40 PP)
Press and hold while locked onto an enemy to charge up to 8 spheres surrounding Redran. Release to launch all spheres at the target.

If there aren't any crowds or enemies with multiple parts around, Burst serves as the best option for dealing with a singular enemy.



Redran Burst can also be used in combination with L/Jumping Dodge, enabling Redran to rain down damage from safety.

Glass

Advanced pet of Redran featuring the same PAs, but with quicker animations, different PP costs, and different mechanics on some PAs. Glass has the option of using Redran’s Rock personality (by upgrading Redran into Glass), or using it’s unique personality, Genius.

Unfortunately, Glass with Rock personality is generally worse than Redran due to the low amount of lasers on Glass Laser and poor PP efficiency on its PAs. Glass with Genius Personality is also rather poor as it just doesn’t compete with higher DPS bossing pets such as Synchro.

Glass is currently available only on JP.

Rock

Provides Glass the bonuses of both of Redran's base personalities and +5% RDR.

Genius

Increases damage against bosses by 20% and greatly increases PP recovery.

Glass Laser (20 PP)
Press and hold to lock on to up to 4 enemies or enemy parts, and let go to launch projectiles. Has a faster animation than Redran Laser.

The reduced number of lockons makes this PA generally worse for mobbing than it’s Redran counterpart, and it’s definitely Glass’ most major limitation. Mobbing with this version of Laser is tough.



Glass Slash (8 PP)
Does everything that Redran Slash does, except with higher damage and a higher PP cost.



Glass Mine (20 PP)
Places a marker that can be detonated by Glass Laser, Burst, or Gravity. Creates a sphere that deals damage over time for a few seconds.

Costs double what Redran Mine does, but deals slightly higher damage.



Glass Gravity (10 PP)
Create a gravitational field drawing in enemies in a small area.

Has all the same functionality as Redran Gravity at a slightly lower PP cost.



Glass Burst (35 PP)
Press and hold while locked onto an enemy to charge up to 8 spheres surrounding Glass. Release to launch all spheres at the target.

Charges stupid quick compared to Redran Burst, but deals 35% less damage. Has lower DPS than Redran Burst.

This costs 5 PP less than Redran Burst, but charges nearly twice as quickly - expect this to chug through your PP a bit.
